The Old Standard

How a number came to define an industry.

The 80-Point Rule

For decades, "specialty coffee" had a clear, quantitative definition. To earn the title, a coffee had to score **80 points or higher** on the SCA's 100-point cupping scale. This score was derived from assessing attributes like aroma, flavor, acidity, and body.

Defect-Free Foundation

Beyond the cup score, the green beans themselves faced strict scrutiny. A 350g sample could have **zero primary defects** (like a full sour bean) and no more than five secondary defects. This dual system created a clear, albeit rigid, benchmark for quality.

This system was pivotal, creating a common language for quality that helped the specialty market grow. However, its intense focus on a numerical score could overshadow other crucial aspects of a coffee's identity, such as its unique varietal, innovative processing, or compelling origin story.

Industry Crossroads

Visualizing the diverse reactions to the CVA.

The introduction of the CVA has been met with a mix of excitement and skepticism. This chart represents the general sentiment from key industry stakeholders, highlighting the perceived benefits versus the concerns.

Producers

Largely optimistic, seeing a chance for better value recognition for their full story, including sustainability and unique processing.

Roasters & Importers

Mixed views. Many appreciate the richer data, while others worry about implementation complexity and how to translate it to consumers.

CQI / Q-Graders

A cautious but engaged stance, viewing the CVA as an "additional system" that coexists with, rather than replaces, the established Q Grader program.

The Path Forward

The evolution to the CVA framework is more than a technical update; it's a reorientation of the industry's values towards greater transparency, inclusivity, and a deeper appreciation for everything that makes a coffee truly special. Its success hinges on continued education, refinement, and effective communication across the entire value chain‚Äîfrom producer to consumer. It's a journey towards a more equitable and sustainable future for coffee.
